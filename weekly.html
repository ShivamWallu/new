Gym Workout Chart

Day 1: Chest and Triceps

Day 1
  • 1. Bench Press (Chest) - 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • 2. Incline Dumbbell Press (Chest) - 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • 3. Chest Flyes (Chest) - 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Day 2: Back and Biceps

Day 2

  • Barbell Bicep Curls (Biceps) - 3 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Hammer Curls (Biceps) - 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Day 3: Legs and Calves

Day 3

  • Leg Press (Legs) - 3 sets of 8-10 reps

  • Standing Calf Raises (Calves) - 4 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Seated Calf Raises (Calves) - 4 sets of 10-12 reps

Day 4: Shoulders and Abs

Day 4

  • Front Raises (Shoulders) - 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Tips for Exercises

Tips
  • Start with a weight that allows you to complete the desired number of reps with proper form. Gradually increase the weight as you get stronger.
  • Focus on controlled movements, especially during the eccentric (lowering) phase of each repetition.
  • Ensure a full range of motion for each exercise to engage the biceps fully.
  • Avoid swinging or using momentum to lift the weights. This can reduce the effectiveness of the exercise and increase the risk of injury.
  • Allow adequate rest between sets (usually 1-2 minutes) to recover and maintain the quality of each set.
  • It's important to balance bicep training with overall upper body and compound exercises for balanced muscle development.
  • Consistency is key. Stick to your bicep workout routine and track your progress over time.
